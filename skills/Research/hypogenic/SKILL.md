# Hypogenic

Automated hypothesis generation and testing using large language models. Use this skill when generating scientific hypotheses from datasets, combining literature insights with empirical data, testing hypotheses against observational data, or conducting systematic hypothesis exploration for research discovery in domains like deception detection, AI content detection, mental health analysis, or other empirical research tasks.
## Instruction
- Analyze the input dataset and existing literature to identify research gaps or unexplored patterns.
- Orchestrate LLMs to synthesize literature insights with empirical data patterns to generate novel, testable hypotheses.
- Categorize generated hypotheses based on feasibility, novelty, and potential scientific impact.
- Design systematic testing protocols to evaluate hypotheses against observational or experimental data.
- Refine hypotheses iteratively based on initial testing results and feedback loops.
- Document the entire exploration process, including rejected hypotheses, to ensure scientific transparency.

## When to Use
- When conducting systematic hypothesis exploration in domains like AI content detection, deception detection, or mental health.
- When needing to combine vast literature repositories with new empirical datasets to spark research ideas.
- When automating the "idea generation" phase of the scientific method for high-throughput research discovery.

## Output
- A set of structured, scientifically grounded hypotheses with rationales and proposed testing methods.
- Summaries of literature-data synthesis highlighting the origin of each research idea.
- Actionable next steps for experimental validation and statistical testing of the top-ranked hypotheses.
