# Task Coordination Strategies

Use this guidance-only skill to design coordinated execution plans across teams. It does not execute tools directly.

## When to use

- You need to split a complex effort into manageable tracks.
- You want dependency clarity and fewer blocked tasks.
- You need a reporting cadence for leadership and stakeholders.

## Core workflows

### 1) Work decomposition

- Break goals into deliverables with clear outcomes.
- Assign accountable owner, due date, and definition of done.
- Separate critical-path tasks from parallelizable tasks.

### 2) Dependency management

- Map prerequisites, handoffs, and downstream impacts.
- Define unblock protocols and escalation deadlines.
- Track dependency health in weekly review cycles.

### 3) Execution governance

- Use a fixed rhythm for planning, status review, and replanning.
- Define status criteria (on track, at risk, blocked).
- Require next action for every at-risk item.

### 4) Communication and alignment

- Provide concise updates by outcome, risk, and decision needed.
- Keep stakeholder asks specific and time-bound.
- Log decisions and scope changes with owner approval.

## Risk controls

- Avoid work inflation without capacity adjustments.
- Prevent hidden blockers by enforcing early escalation.
- Keep handoff quality high with explicit acceptance criteria.

## Output format

When asked for help, provide:

- A dependency map template.
- A weekly coordination meeting agenda.
- A status reporting format with intervention triggers.
