# Investor and Board Leadership

Use this guidance-only skill for executive strategy, investor relations, and board operations. It does not run scripts or commands.

## When to use

- You need a structured board narrative with clear decisions and asks.
- You want a repeatable investor update cadence.
- You need risk-aware frameworks for major strategic decisions.

## Core workflows

### 1) Strategic decision support

- Define the decision statement, alternatives, assumptions, and constraints.
- Evaluate options by impact, risk, reversibility, and resource demand.
- Document recommended action, owner, timeline, and success metrics.

### 2) Board governance

- Prepare concise board packets: KPI snapshot, major risks, key decisions.
- Separate informational items from approval-required items.
- Track action items from board meetings with named owners and due dates.

### 3) Investor communications

- Publish a regular update cadence (monthly or quarterly).
- Cover performance, risks, strategic progress, and explicit asks.
- Keep message consistency across board, investors, and leadership team.

### 4) Fundraising readiness

- Maintain data room hygiene and ownership by document section.
- Keep financial assumptions, model versions, and scenario logic aligned.
- Prepare responses for key diligence themes (market, margins, retention, runway).

## Risk controls

- Avoid optimistic projections without scenario ranges.
- Escalate material deviations from plan with corrective actions.
- Keep governance records complete for decisions and follow-through.

## Output format

When asked for help, provide:

- A board memo outline.
- An investor update template.
- A strategic decision brief with risk-adjusted options.
