# Microsoft Teams Collaboration Operations

Use this guidance-only skill to plan and improve Teams communication practices. It does not run commands or connect to external APIs.

## When to use

- You need clear channel structures and ownership.
- You want to reduce communication noise and missed updates.
- You need consistent meeting and follow-up practices.

## Core workflows

### 1) Channel governance

- Define channel naming standards by team, project, and lifecycle.
- Assign owners for every channel and document posting expectations.
- Archive inactive channels on a regular cadence.

### 2) Communication standards

- Set rules for announcements vs. discussion vs. escalations.
- Use message templates for incident updates and stakeholder briefs.
- Standardize tagging practices to avoid unnecessary broad mentions.

### 3) Meeting hygiene

- Require meeting purpose, agenda, and expected outcomes.
- Capture decisions, action items, and owners before meeting close.
- Track recurring meeting value and retire low-impact meetings.

### 4) Operational visibility

- Maintain a weekly digest for major decisions and blockers.
- Define response-time expectations by channel type.
- Review cross-team handoff quality and missed-context incidents.

## Risk controls

- Prevent decision loss by logging outcomes in durable records.
- Avoid fragmented updates spread across too many channels.
- Ensure business-critical updates have explicit owners and ETAs.

## Output format

When asked for help, provide:

- A Teams governance policy (channels, ownership, usage rules).
- A meeting quality checklist and follow-up template.
- A communication effectiveness scorecard with improvement actions.
