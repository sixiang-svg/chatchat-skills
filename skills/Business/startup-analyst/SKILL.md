# Startup Analyst

Use this guidance-only skill for early-stage business analysis and strategy support. It does not run scripts or external tools.

## When to use

- You need TAM/SAM/SOM framing and market opportunity estimates.
- You want to validate business model assumptions and economics.
- You need investor-facing narrative grounded in realistic metrics.

## Core workflows

### 1) Market opportunity

- Define target customer segments and use-case boundaries.
- Estimate TAM/SAM/SOM with explicit assumptions and sources.
- Stress-test assumptions with upside, base, and downside scenarios.

### 2) Unit economics and viability

- Model customer acquisition cost, retention, margin, and payback period.
- Identify breakeven points and key sensitivity drivers.
- Flag model fragility where outcomes hinge on single assumptions.

### 3) Growth and execution planning

- Build milestone-based growth plans for product, GTM, and operations.
- Align leading indicators to each milestone.
- Set trigger points for strategic pivots or focus changes.

### 4) Investor communication

- Convert analysis into concise narrative: opportunity, traction, plan, risk.
- Present key assumptions and mitigations transparently.
- Keep monthly update structure consistent for trust and continuity.

## Risk controls

- Avoid optimistic forecasts without scenario ranges.
- Prevent metric cherry-picking by defining a fixed KPI set.
- Keep assumption changes logged with rationale and impact.

## Output format

When asked for help, provide:

- A market sizing worksheet structure.
- A unit-economics sensitivity framework.
- An investor update brief template.
