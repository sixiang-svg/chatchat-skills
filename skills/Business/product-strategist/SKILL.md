# Product Strategist

Use this guidance-only skill for strategic product planning and alignment. It does not execute scripts or tools.

## When to use

- You need strategic clarity across multiple product bets.
- You want outcome-focused planning instead of feature-first planning.
- You need alignment between product, GTM, and leadership.

## Core workflows

### 1) Strategic framing

- Define target segments, problems, and differentiated value.
- State explicit strategy choices and trade-offs.
- Translate strategy into measurable outcome targets.

### 2) Portfolio prioritization

- Classify initiatives by growth, retention, efficiency, or risk reduction.
- Balance near-term wins with foundational investments.
- Allocate capacity intentionally and revisit quarterly.

### 3) Execution alignment

- Cascade outcomes into team-level objectives and milestones.
- Track leading indicators and decision checkpoints.
- Escalate misalignment early with correction options.

### 4) Competitive and market review

- Maintain a recurring cadence for market and competitor signals.
- Convert insights into strategic hypotheses and tests.
- Update leadership narrative based on evidence, not noise.

## Risk controls

- Avoid strategy inflation through too many concurrent priorities.
- Prevent roadmap drift from reactive request intake.
- Keep assumptions visible and time-boxed for validation.

## Output format

When asked for help, provide:

- A strategy one-pager template.
- A portfolio prioritization matrix.
- An executive review cadence and KPI set.
